WHAT IS FOOD WASTE?

Food waste is the act of throwing away food that can still be used. An innocent mistake everyone is guilty of from time to time. However, when large bodies of people are making the same mistakes, that's when it becomes a concern. From your wallet all the way to the very climate we live in, food waste just causes unnecessary problems.

Before the food even enters your home, you are probably carrying food you won't even eat, so money is already being wasted. Once the food to be wasted enters, it either goes unopened until it "expires" or it gets thrown out due to a loss in preferred quality.

The "expiration" date is the most common reason people throw away food. People seem to have this misconception that they shoud avoid this food because it is no longer edible or safe. The truth is, a lot of the food getting thrown away are still perfectly safe to eat. "Sell by" is meant to tell store employees when to pull their items off the shelves, regardless of its current state, and "best before" is when the supplier thinks the food will begin to lose its fresh feature.

As for the loss in preferred quality, even if you have a perfectly valid reason to not eat the food in its state, there are still other options. You can:

  • You can use the ingredients in other ways (ground stale bread to bread crumbs to make breaded chicken)
  • Use the food for household chores (pour sour milk over garden to provide calcium)
  • Give the food to others who may like it
  • Give it to a local farmer to feed to their animals
  • And many other options!

    • At this point you may have pointed out compost and how it should prevent food from being truly wasted. What you may not know, is that composting has consequences. The first being that as your waste waits outside to be picked up, it attracts vermin and bugs. The second being, that it gets added to the already gigantic landfills. And thirdly, as the large body of food decomposes, it releases a huge amount of methane, a greenhouse gas, into the atmosphere.

    To recap, food waste eats away money unnecessarily, takes up space, gets thrown away even though there are other options, attracts vermin, and contributes to climate change. But to put things into perspective, let's look at the numbers.
